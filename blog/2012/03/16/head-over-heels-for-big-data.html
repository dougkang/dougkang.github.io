Head over heels for Big Data

  • Blog
  • Projects

16 Mar 2012

One of the things that I have been starting to get really interested about is Big Data.  It’s been a casual relationship at first - an article here, a lecture there.  But Avinaush Kaushik’s keynote at the Strata 2012 has pushed me head over heels for the whole thing.  The keynote, called “A Big Data Imperative: Driving Big Action” is definitely a really good watch; He is an engaging, passionate, and charismatic person talking about the thing that he is most passionate about:

Kaushik’s vision is this:

To create a data democracy…

The amount of data being made available is so vast that the old model of having “data princelings” cooking up the data into something that is palatable for others is no longer scalable.  Instead, everyone will need to get their hands dirty and deal with data.

… sans data puking …

Again, the vast amount of data available would mean that we have to be smarter about looking at the data, meaning:

  1. Smarter Sorting: when analyzing the data, we tend to look at the extremities of the data, which tend to be very sparse.  To gain insight, we shouldn’t be looking at this data, but instead at the meaty middle.

  2. Predict, Mine, Correlate: we need to admit that we will never be able to identify all unknown unknowns without help.  We need to employ the help of our machines to provide better starting points to be able to help us identify and act.

.. which can empower action everyday.
