NES, Nostalgia

  • Blog
  • Projects

17 May 2010

So today I got in the mail, my very own Nintendo.  No no, not a Wii, but the original NES.  In an action driven by pure nostalgia, I decided that I will make it my hobby to collect games for the NES.  None of the crappy ones, though.  I remember going to my church friend’s house (his name was Michael) just to play Double Dragon on the NES.  My first exposure to a video game console.  Those were the days, huh?  Remember when you had to blow on the cartridges to get them to work?  Back then, I thought those graphics were the ish and here we are in the HD world.

So the NES came with one game - Mario Brothers.  Yeah baby,  this is as OG as it can get.  The console itself has a new 72 pin connector installed, so the game plays with no blowing required.  A couple more games are on the way, namely Ninja Gaiden and Double Dragon (how could I not?).

The first video game console I ever owned was a Super Nintendo.  My dad brought it home after going on a business trip to Oregon.  Man was that the happiest day of my life!  Opening it up and playing Super Mario World for the first time.  And when I beat Bowser for the first time, we literally had a party to celebrate.  Since then, I’ve had just about every Nintendo console/handheld created, with the exception of the NES (which I now have) and VirtualBoy (but that thing was a load of crap anyways, so no thanks).

I’ve been very nostalgic lately, thinking about the good old days when life was simpler… You have to admit, even though technology is simplifying our lives, it’s still pretty darn complicated.
