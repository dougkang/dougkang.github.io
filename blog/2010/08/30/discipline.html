30 Aug 2010

Lately, I’ve been realizing the lack of discipline I have in just about everything I do, which is weird because I always saw myself as a rather disciplined individual.  I finally shook myself out of denial when I realized that, for every 5 minutes I was trying to read up on learning Erlang, I would switch to a different tab and check my fantasy football team or something else completely random. … Just now I just switched to a new tab to check what was on Woot! today.  I realized that I just can’t get myself to focus for more than 5 minutes without getting distracted by something completely and utterly orthogonal to what I am working on.  My last attempt on learning how to learn (I was reading a book called “Refactoring Your Wetware”) further fueled my ADD-like behavior by telling that I have to let my subconscious do the work and to take breaks every so often to process what I had just learned.  Somehow, I got it into my head that it’s good to take breaks and, moreover, it’s even better to take breaks more often and obviously, this is NOT the right thing to do.

So, in a spur-of-the-moment kind of motivation, I have decided to take steps to make myself more disciplined in a couple areas of my life.  First and foremost, I need to do my daily QTs.  Second, I need to take a disciplined approach to my career.  Finally, I have to take a disciplined approach to my life.  I accomplish these things by doing the following:

1. QT Daily - ‘Nuff said. 2. Use a notebook to trap my random tangents so that I can maintain my train of thought.   Basically, I will keep a small notebook by my side at all times and whenever I lose focus because of some random thing, I will write it down. 3. Morning Meditation - 15 minutes minimum 4. Morning and Post-work memory dumps - 3 pages total of WHATEVER COMES TO MY HEAD 5. Wake up and sleep at the same time daily - Times TBD 6. Exercise regularly - Type of Exercise TBD

I know, I know.  The list gets vaguer as the numbers increase.  It shows how much I thought about the later ones.  In any case, I’ll make things clearer as time goes by and I accomplish the higher entries on the list.  In any case, let’s do this!
