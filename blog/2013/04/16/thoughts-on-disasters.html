Look for the helpers

16 Apr 2013

In light of the attack that happened during the Boston Marathon, I thought I’d store away for future reference one of the quotes that has been passed around quite a bit. Mister Rogers, on the advice that his mother gave him when he was young:

When I was a boy and I would see scary things in the news, my mother would say to me, “Look for the helpers. You will always find people who are helping.”” To this day, especially in times of “disaster,” I remember my mother’s words and I am always comforted by realizing that there are still so many helpers – so many caring people in this world.

In his own words:

An amazing quote from an amazing man. Here are 10 more amazing things I found out about him:

  1. He started out in TV because he hated it and thought that it could be used better as an instrument to nurture those who would watch and listen.
  2. Every zippered cardigan he wore on Mister Roger’s Neighborhood was knitted by his mother. A red one is on display in the Smithsonian and a “Treasure of American History”.
  3. He wrote his own songs.
  4. Mister Rogers was the same off-camera as he was on-camera, stating that “One of the greatest gifts you can give anybody is the gift of your honest self. I also believe that kids can spot a phony a mile away.”
  5. Mister Rogers never did any commercial endorsements of any kind, though frequently did non-profit endorsements for organizations dedicated to learning.
  6. In 1969, when Nixon threatened to cut a $20 Million proposed budget, Mister Rogers appeared before the US Senate Subcommittee on Communications and delivered a passionate plea and the cut was overturned.
  7. Mister Rogers testimony was considered in the right to record shows in 1979, going against the views of others in the television industry. His reason? Children could record his shows and watch them with their family.
  8. He is ranked 35th in TV Guide’s Greatest TV Stars of All Time.
  9. The traffic light decoration on the show is always on yellow - a reminder to kids and parents to slow down.
  10. Mister Rogers began airing in 1968 and ran for 895 episodes, with the last set of new episodes being taped in December 2000. Talk about dedication.

It’s not often that a man can live a simple life full of integrity and compassion and yet make a huge impact on the a nation.
