A feasibility exercise

14 Apr 2013

There has been something I wanted to tinker with for a while now that my coworker thought of and today I finally got around to doing it. I simplified the problem and boiled it down to this:

Given a 2 week slice of historical stock data (start, high, low, close, volume), can I predict whether or not the stock will turn a profit in the next 1 week?

It seemed simple enough that countless people have probably already thought of and tried something like it. And since every other person is not a millionaire, you could have colored me a bit skeptical that this would actually be viable. Nevertheless, I figured it was an interesting exercise and I thought I’d make a rough first cut to see if it was worthwhile pursuing.

I approached this as a classification problem, though in hindsight, I probably shouldn’t have. I figured 1 week is enough time to let the stock “bake”, but not enough for me to lose alot of money if things go badly. As such, I concluded that if I could just pick more profitable 1 week windows than nonprofitable ones, on the whole, I would make money. For my data, I took the 10 years of historical data for about 10 semi-randomly chosen stocks and I started tinkering.

Given the (relatively) small dataset, I figured I could probably use an svm to do the classification for me, with the 2 week slice of historical data as my attributes. I kept the stock data seperate, meaning that I used only the stock’s data to build the svms for that particular stock. Today was just the first cut, so its still very rough. I ran my test set through the svms and then calculated the actual per-share profit for the results:
